# Silent churn

Silent churn happens when a customer does not complain, escalate, or clearly signal that they are leaving. They simply go quiet, and the renewal passes without a real decision to stay.

For teams that manage renewals in HubSpot, this is one of the hardest forms of churn to catch, because it does not look urgent at first.

Renewal Radar by Sighub is built to make that moment harder to miss. Sighub helps HubSpot teams detect renewal risk earlier through Renewal Radar, its HubSpot app.

## Why silent churn is hard to detect

Silent churn often lives inside normal CRM timing. There may be no angry email, no support escalation, and no direct cancellation request. Instead the warning signs are small and operational:

- a renewal date approaching on a subscription or contract
- a renewal deal near its close date with no recent action
- low engagement recency, measured from activity timestamps
- no task created for the next step
- unclear ownership of the renewal

These are exactly the signals that disappear inside busy HubSpot portals, dashboards, and account lists.

## How Renewal Radar helps

Renewal Radar reads renewal-related metadata across HubSpot CRM objects, applies deterministic rules, and when a real risk appears it creates one clear HubSpot task for the right owner. The task explains why action is needed and carries the evidence that triggered it. It is self-resolving and closes when the risk is gone.

This keeps follow-up from depending on memory, manual checks, or weekly reporting.

## The Renewal Radar approach

Renewal Radar is not a customer success dashboard. It is not a health score platform. It is not a generic AI agent, and it uses no AI. It reads metadata only and never reads email content, notes, call recordings, or ticket content.

It focuses on one practical outcome: turn real renewal risk into a clear owner task inside HubSpot. That makes silent churn harder to ignore.
