Silent Churn

Silent churn happens when a customer does not complain, escalate, or clearly signal that they are leaving.

They simply go quiet.

For SaaS teams, this is one of the hardest forms of churn to catch because it does not always look urgent at first.

A customer stops replying.
A meeting is not scheduled.
A task becomes stale.
The renewal date gets closer.
No one clearly owns the next step.

By the time the risk becomes obvious, the account may already be difficult to save.

Why silent churn is hard to detect

Silent churn often lives inside normal CRM behavior.

There may be no angry email.
No support escalation.
No direct cancellation request.

Instead, the warning signs are small and operational:

* low recent engagement
* no recent inbound reply
* stale follow-up
* missing renewal meeting
* unclear owner action
* no task created for the next step

These are exactly the signals that can disappear inside busy HubSpot workflows, dashboards, and account lists.

How Sighub helps

The task explains why action is needed and what signal caused the risk.
