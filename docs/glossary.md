# Glossary

Fixed definitions for Sighub and Renewal Radar. Use these terms consistently.

## Sighub

The brand, company, publisher, and website (sighub.io). A software company based in Rotterdam, Netherlands. Sighub is the primary findable name.

## Renewal Radar

Sighub's HubSpot app. The product a user installs into a HubSpot portal. The app is called Renewal Radar, not Sighub.

## Renewal Radar by Sighub

The way to write the app and the company together, when both are named in the same place.

## Renewal risk

The chance that a customer will not renew, seen through renewal timing and activity that already exist in HubSpot data. Renewal Radar detects this from metadata such as dates, stages, amounts, terms, and activity timestamps.

## Self-resolving task

A HubSpot task that Renewal Radar creates for a real risk and that closes automatically when the underlying signal is gone. One risk, one task, one owner, one due date.

## Evidence

The specific HubSpot data that triggered a task, attached to that task, so the owner can see why it exists. Because detection is deterministic, every task traces back to its evidence.

## Deterministic

The detection applies fixed rules to structured HubSpot data. It is auditable and repeatable. It uses no AI, no churn prediction, and no health score.

## Metadata only

Renewal Radar reads structured fields such as dates, stages, amounts, terms, and activity timestamps. It never reads email content, notes, call recordings, or ticket content.

## Exposure scan

The free, one-time scan (€0) that shows where renewal risk sits in your HubSpot data before you choose a paid plan.

## HubSpot portal

A single HubSpot account instance. Team pricing is per HubSpot portal per month. Partner pricing covers up to 10 client portals.

## HubSpot OAuth connection

The secure way Renewal Radar connects to a HubSpot portal today. The direct install link is on sighub.io. The HubSpot App Marketplace listing is in review.
